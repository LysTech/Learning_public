## Summary
This work presents a method for reconstructing mental imagery from human brain activity using deep neural networks and Bayesian estimation. The approach decodes fMRI signals to generate visual images corresponding to what a person is mentally imagining, advancing the field of neural decoding for visual cognition.

## Key points
- Reconstructs visual mental imagery directly from fMRI brain activity
- Employs deep neural network-based Bayesian estimation for decoding
- Demonstrates neural decoding of subjective visual experiences
- Contributes to understanding the neural basis of mental visualization
