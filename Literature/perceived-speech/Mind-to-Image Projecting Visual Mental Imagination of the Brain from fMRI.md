## Summary
This paper presents a method to reconstruct visual mental imagery from fMRI brain scans by projecting neural activity into image space using diffusion models. The work leverages the semantic structure of diffusion latent spaces to smoothly sample regions of the visual imagination space, enabling generation of images corresponding to what a person is mentally imagining.

## Key points
- Reconstructs visual mental imagery directly from fMRI using diffusion model latent spaces
- Leverages the smooth semantic structure of diffusion models to sample visual imagination space
- Demonstrates that brain activity can be projected into a continuous image generation space
- Connects neuroscience and generative models for understanding visual perception and imagination
