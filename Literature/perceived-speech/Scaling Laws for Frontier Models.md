## Summary
This paper investigates scaling laws for large language models, examining how model performance improves with increased parameters, compute, and data. The work provides empirical evidence and theoretical insights into optimal allocation of resources for training frontier models.

## Key points
- Empirical scaling laws governing model performance as a function of model size and compute
- Analysis of optimal resource allocation between parameters and training compute
- Implications for predicting performance of frontier models during training
