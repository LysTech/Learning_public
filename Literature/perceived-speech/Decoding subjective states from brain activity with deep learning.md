## Summary
This study demonstrates the use of deep learning to decode subjective mental states from fMRI brain activity data, achieving >65% classification accuracy with 2000 fMRI samples. The work explores the feasibility of scaling state decoding approaches to alternative neuroimaging modalities.

## Key points
- Deep learning achieves >65% accuracy in decoding subjective states from fMRI at 2000 samples
- Demonstrates scalability and robustness of decoding across different neuroimaging modalities
- Relevant for translating findings from fMRI to more accessible imaging techniques like fNIRS
