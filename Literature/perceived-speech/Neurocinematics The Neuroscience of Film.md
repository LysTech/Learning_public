## Summary
This foundational work demonstrates that viewing naturalistic films evokes similar brain activity patterns across subjects, with the degree of neural alignment varying by film content. The paper introduces the framework of neurocinematics, combining neuroscience with film analysis to understand how cinematic narrative drives synchronized neural responses.

## Key points
- Different films evoke varying degrees of inter-subject neural synchrony in fMRI measurements
- Stimuli that constrain viewer interpretation (like films) produce more consistent neural responses than open-ended stimuli
- Neural alignment correlates with film narrative structure and emotional content
- Establishes neurocinematics as a framework for studying how naturalistic, complex stimuli drive brain activity
