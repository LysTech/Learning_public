## Summary
This paper investigates empirical scaling laws relating model size, dataset size, and compute to language model performance. The authors derive simple power-law relationships that enable accurate prediction of model performance without full training.

## Key points
- Derives power-law scaling relationships for language model performance as a function of model size, dataset size, and compute budget
- Enables accurate performance prediction without requiring full model training
- Provides practical guidance for optimal allocation of compute across model and data scaling
- Findings demonstrate that language model performance follows predictable scaling patterns across a wide range of scales
