## Summary
This paper investigates potential biases in popular open-domain question answering benchmarks, examining whether they reflect genuine QA capabilities or are subject to dataset-specific artifacts and limitations.

## Key points
- Analyzes bias and limitations in widely-used QA benchmarks
- Evaluates whether benchmarks adequately test generalizable QA abilities
- Suggests modern benchmarks may not address contemporary information retrieval challenges
- Provides critical assessment of benchmark design choices
