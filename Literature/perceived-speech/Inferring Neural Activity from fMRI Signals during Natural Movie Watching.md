## Summary
This study demonstrates that semantic information about visual stimuli can be decoded from fMRI brain activity patterns using machine learning. The authors developed predictive models of neural responses during natural movie watching and showed that semantic representations are distributed across cortical areas.

## Key points
- Semantic information from visual stimuli can be decoded from distributed fMRI activation patterns
- Machine learning models trained on brain responses predict neural activity to novel stimuli
- Visual semantic processing involves multiple cortical areas with complementary representations
- Demonstrates feasibility of brain-based decoding for complex natural stimuli
