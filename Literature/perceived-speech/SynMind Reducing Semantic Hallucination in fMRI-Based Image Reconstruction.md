## Summary
This paper addresses semantic hallucination in fMRI-based image reconstruction by proposing SynMind, a method that synthesizes brain activity to reduce spurious activations and improve reconstruction fidelity. The approach leverages synthetic fMRI signals to guide the reconstruction process and enhance the quality of decoded visual content.

## Key points
- Identifies and addresses semantic hallucination problem in brain-to-image decoding from fMRI
- Proposes synthetic fMRI generation to reduce spurious neural activations
- Improves reconstruction quality and semantic consistency compared to baseline methods
- Demonstrates enhanced generalization across different subjects and visual categories
