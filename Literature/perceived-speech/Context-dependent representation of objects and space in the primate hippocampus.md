## Summary
This paper examines how the primate hippocampus encodes objects and spatial information in a context-dependent manner, demonstrating that neural representations are fundamentally shaped by behavioral context and environmental conditions.

## Key points
- Hippocampal neurons show flexible, context-dependent firing patterns for the same objects and locations
- Neural representation depends critically on task demands and behavioral state
- Context-dependent coding may support cognitive flexibility and memory integration
- Evidence from single-unit recordings in behaving primates performing multiple tasks
