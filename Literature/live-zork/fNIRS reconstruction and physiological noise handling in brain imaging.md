## Summary
This work addresses fNIRS data reconstruction pipelines and strategies for handling physiological noise artifacts such as Meyer waves, comparing approaches for preprocessing and reconstruction on individual brain anatomy versus template-based methods.

## Key points
- Discusses fNIRS reconstruction using individual brain anatomy versus template brains (Colin27)
- Outlines preprocessing pipeline including data trimming, bad channel removal, histogram correction, and moment computation
- Addresses physiological noise removal strategies including short-distance channel regression and integrated noise correction during reconstruction
- Compares MCXLAB-based Jacobian computation for TD-fNIRS against intensity-based approaches
