## Summary
This paper investigates how alignment between the representational spaces of teachers and learners affects the effectiveness of machine teaching. The authors develop theoretical frameworks and empirical methods to measure representational alignment and demonstrate that greater alignment leads to more efficient teaching and better generalization performance.

## Key points
- Representational alignment between teacher and learner models is crucial for effective machine teaching
- Proposes metrics to quantify alignment in learned representations across different architectures
- Shows that aligned teaching strategies achieve faster learning and better transfer to new tasks
- Bridges cognitive science insights about human teaching with machine learning optimization
- Provides practical methods for improving teaching efficiency in AI systems
