## Summary
This study investigates how older adults perform on model-based reversal learning tasks, finding that they fail to form stable task representations compared to younger adults. The research suggests age-related deficits in flexible learning and adaptation during probabilistic decision-making.

## Key points
- Older adults show impaired ability to form stable task representations during reversal learning
- Deficits are specific to model-based (flexible) inference rather than model-free learning
- Age-related changes in task representation stability contribute to reduced behavioral flexibility
- Findings have implications for understanding cognitive aging and decision-making decline
