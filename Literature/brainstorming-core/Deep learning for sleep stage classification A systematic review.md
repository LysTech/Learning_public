## Summary
A comprehensive systematic review of deep learning approaches applied to automated sleep stage classification from polysomnography signals, covering state-of-the-art methods, datasets, and performance metrics.

## Key points
- Surveys deep learning architectures (CNNs, RNNs, attention mechanisms) for sleep stage classification
- Discusses challenges in data preprocessing, feature extraction, and inter-rater variability
- Reviews publicly available sleep datasets and their characteristics
- Identifies gaps in generalization across different patient populations and recording devices
