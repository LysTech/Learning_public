## Summary
This paper proposes a convolutional neural network-based method for reconstructing missing EEG channels in limited brain-computer interfaces, with applications to neuromarketing. The approach enables robust EEG signal processing when only a subset of electrodes are available.

## Key points
- CNN-based approach for EEG channel reconstruction from incomplete electrode sets
- Enables practical BCI systems with reduced number of sensors
- Designed for neuromarketing applications where full electrode coverage may be impractical
- Addresses the challenge of working with limited EEG data in commercial or field settings
