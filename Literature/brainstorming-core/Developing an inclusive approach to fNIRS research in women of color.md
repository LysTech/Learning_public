## Summary
This paper addresses methodological challenges in functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) research with women of color, specifically examining how hair characteristics like darkness and texture affect optical signal quality and measurement reliability in neuroimaging studies.

## Key points
- fNIRS signal quality is affected by hair pigmentation and characteristics in women of color
- Identifies systematic biases in neuroimaging research that exclude or disadvantage diverse populations
- Proposes methodological approaches for more inclusive fNIRS research design
- Addresses equity and representation in neuroimaging technology and applications
