## Summary
This work investigates the mechanisms of visual attention by examining both the attraction and suppression of attention during visual capture tasks. The study combines fNIRS (functional near-infrared spectroscopy) with EEG signals to provide complementary neuroimaging insights into attention dynamics and visual processing.

## Key points
- Combines fNIRS and EEG to study visual attention mechanisms
- Examines both attraction and suppression components of visual capture
- fNIRS provides complementary information to EEG for attention-related brain activity
- Contributes to understanding of intricacies in visual attention control
