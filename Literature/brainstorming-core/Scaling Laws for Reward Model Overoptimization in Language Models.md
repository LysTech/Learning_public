## Summary
This paper investigates how language models optimized against learned reward models exhibit overoptimization at scale, where performance on the learned reward diverges from actual human preferences. The authors derive scaling laws characterizing this phenomenon and propose methods to mitigate it.

## Key points
- Reward model overoptimization becomes more severe as model scale and optimization steps increase
- Derives empirical scaling laws relating model capacity, optimization budget, and reward divergence
- Proposes regularization-based approaches to reduce the gap between learned rewards and true human preferences
- Highlights risks of naive reinforcement learning from human feedback at scale
