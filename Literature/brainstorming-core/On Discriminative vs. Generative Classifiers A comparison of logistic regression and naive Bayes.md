## Summary
This paper provides a theoretical and empirical comparison between discriminative classifiers (logistic regression) and generative classifiers (naive Bayes), analyzing their asymptotic and finite-sample properties and demonstrating that discriminative learning has lower asymptotic error but generative learning can be better with small training sets.

## Key points
- Compares discriminative (logistic regression) vs. generative (naive Bayes) classifiers theoretically and empirically
- Discriminative classifiers have lower asymptotic error rates
- Generative classifiers can achieve better performance with limited training data
- Provides practical guidance for choosing between classifier types based on data availability
