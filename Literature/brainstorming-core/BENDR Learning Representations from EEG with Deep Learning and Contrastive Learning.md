## Summary
BENDR presents a self-supervised learning approach using transformers and contrastive learning to learn generalizable representations from large-scale EEG data. The method addresses the challenge of learning from unlabeled EEG by employing masked modeling and contrastive objectives, enabling better transfer to downstream tasks.

## Key points
- Uses transformer architecture with contrastive self-supervised learning for EEG representation learning
- Addresses the challenge of learning from massive amounts of unlabeled EEG data
- Employs masked sequence modeling as an alternative to autoencoders to avoid learning noise
- Learns transferable representations that generalize across subjects and EEG-based tasks
- Demonstrates the effectiveness of self-supervised pretraining on downstream EEG classification tasks
