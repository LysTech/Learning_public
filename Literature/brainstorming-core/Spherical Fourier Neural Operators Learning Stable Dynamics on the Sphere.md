## Summary
This paper introduces Spherical Fourier Neural Operators (SFNOs), a framework for learning stable dynamics on spherical domains using Fourier-based spectral methods. The approach extends neural operators to non-Euclidean geometries, enabling efficient modeling of global phenomena such as weather and climate dynamics while maintaining equivariance and stability properties.

## Key points
- Extends Fourier Neural Operators to spherical geometry using spectral methods on the sphere
- Provides stable and geometrically equivariant learning framework for global dynamical systems
- Applicable to weather and climate modeling without requiring domain flattening or regional decomposition
- Leverages spherical harmonic transforms for efficient computation on spherical data
