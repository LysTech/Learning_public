## Summary
This paper introduces parallel transport convolution, a novel convolutional operation designed for neural networks on Riemannian manifolds. The method extends standard CNN convolutions to curved spaces by leveraging parallel transport, enabling effective feature learning on non-Euclidean geometries such as spheres and other manifolds.

## Key points
- Proposes parallel transport convolution as a generalization of standard convolutions to Riemannian manifolds
- Enables convolutional neural networks to naturally operate on curved geometries without local flattening approximations
- Applicable to various manifold types including spheres, providing theoretical foundation for geometric deep learning
- Relevant for applications where data naturally resides on manifolds, such as spherical signals or curved biological surfaces
