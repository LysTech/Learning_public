## Summary
This paper investigates scaling laws governing reward model overoptimization in language model alignment, analyzing how model size, reward model quality, and optimization intensity affect the degradation of alignment performance.

## Key points
- Identifies empirical scaling laws predicting reward model overoptimization across different model scales
- Shows how reward model quality and training intensity interact to determine alignment performance
- Provides theoretical framework for understanding trade-offs in RLHF-based alignment
- Offers practical guidance for scaling alignment techniques to larger language models
