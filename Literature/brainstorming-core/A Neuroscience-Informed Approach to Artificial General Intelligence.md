## Summary
This paper presents a neuroscience-informed perspective on developing artificial general intelligence, drawing on principles from neurobiology to guide AI research and development. It argues that understanding the brain's computational strategies is essential for building more capable and robust AI systems.

## Key points
- Brain-inspired principles can inform the design of AGI systems
- Neuroscience offers insights into learning, memory, and generalization mechanisms
- Current deep learning approaches miss key organizational principles from biological neural systems
- Integration of neuroscience knowledge may accelerate progress toward artificial general intelligence
