## Summary
ComBat is a statistical method for removing batch effects from high-dimensional data, widely used in neuroimaging to harmonize measurements across different scanners and acquisition sessions while preserving biological signal.

## Key points
- Removes batch effects (e.g., scanner-related variability) from neuroimaging measurements
- Preserves biological variation while correcting for technical artifacts
- Applicable to harmonizing skull thickness and other structural measurements across different imaging protocols
- Addresses session-to-session and scanner-to-scanner variability in longitudinal studies
