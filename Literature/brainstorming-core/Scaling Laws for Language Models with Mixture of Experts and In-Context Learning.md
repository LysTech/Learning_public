## Summary
This paper investigates scaling laws for language models that employ mixture of experts (MoE) architectures combined with in-context learning capabilities. The work provides empirical analysis of how model performance scales with compute, parameters, and training data when using conditional computation through expert routing.

## Key points
- Empirical scaling laws for mixture of experts language models
- Analysis of interaction between expert sparsity and in-context learning
- Practical guidance for efficient model scaling with conditional computation
- Comparison with dense transformer scaling behavior
