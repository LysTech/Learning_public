## Summary
This paper investigates the phenomenon of grokking—delayed generalization in neural networks—by analyzing how networks solve parity problems. The authors propose that grokking occurs when the network gradually learns to exploit a specific feature structure rather than memorizing, leading to sudden performance jumps on unseen data.

## Key points
- Explains grokking as a slow discovery of feature-based solutions to parity tasks
- Networks transition from memorization to generalization through learning of higher-order feature interactions
- Provides mechanistic insights into why generalization is delayed despite early training convergence
- Demonstrates that the grokking phenomenon is related to how networks decompile memorization strategies into generalizable patterns
