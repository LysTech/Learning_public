## Summary
This study examines the relationship between dopamine transporter availability in the brain and the severity of depressive symptoms in human subjects, using neuroimaging to investigate potential neurobiological mechanisms underlying depression.

## Key points
- Dopamine transporter availability inversely correlates with depressive symptom severity
- Findings suggest a neurobiological basis for depression involving dopaminergic dysfunction
- Study uses PET imaging to measure dopamine system integrity in depressed populations
