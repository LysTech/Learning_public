## Summary
This work demonstrates that adversarial examples exist in the audio domain, showing that imperceptible perturbations to audio signals can cause state-of-the-art speech recognition systems to transcribe arbitrary target phrases. The authors develop optimization-based attacks and analyze defenses against such adversarial audio.

## Key points
- First demonstration of practical adversarial examples for audio/speech recognition systems
- Imperceptible perturbations can cause misrecognition of arbitrary target phrases
- Optimization-based attack method using loss functions from the recognition pipeline
- Evaluation against real-world speech recognition systems shows vulnerability
- Analysis of potential defenses and limitations of adversarial robustness in audio domain
