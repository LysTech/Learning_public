## Summary
This paper presents a framework for improving functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) signal quality by systematically incorporating physiological noise measurements. The approach addresses a key challenge in fNIRS data analysis: determining when and how to include systemic physiology information to enhance brain signal extraction.

## Key points
- Proposes a systematic framework for incorporating systemic physiology into fNIRS analysis
- Addresses the challenge of physiological noise contamination in fNIRS signals
- Improves brain signal extraction by optimizing physiological noise inclusion
- Published in Neurophotonics, a leading journal for neuroimaging techniques
