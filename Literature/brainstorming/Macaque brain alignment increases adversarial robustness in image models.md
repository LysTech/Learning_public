## Summary
This work investigates how aligning artificial neural networks with macaque brain representations improves their adversarial robustness. The study demonstrates that training image models to match neural activity patterns in macaque visual cortex leads to enhanced resistance against adversarial attacks.

## Key points
- Aligning image models with macaque visual cortex representations improves adversarial robustness
- Brain-inspired constraints provide an alternative regularization approach to standard adversarial training
- Suggests that biological vision systems may have evolved robust computational principles
- Demonstrates transfer of robustness properties from biological alignment across different threat models
