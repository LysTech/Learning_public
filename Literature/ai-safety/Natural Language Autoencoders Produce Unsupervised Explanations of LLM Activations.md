## Summary
This work applies natural language autoencoder techniques to interpret LLM internal activations by learning unsupervised explanations of Claude's residual stream representations. The approach treats transformer hidden states analogously to brain data, enabling direct decoding of model internals into human-interpretable natural language descriptions.

## Key points
- Uses natural language autoencoders to decode LLM residual stream activations into interpretable explanations
- Treats hidden layer activations as 'neural data' analogous to brain imaging for interpretability
- Provides unsupervised method for understanding what information is encoded at different network layers
- Potentially applicable to neuroscience methods for understanding biological neural systems
