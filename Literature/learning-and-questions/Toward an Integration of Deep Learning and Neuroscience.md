## Key points
- Deep learning and neuroscience address complementary questions: neuroscience focuses on implementation details while ML focuses on algorithmic function
- Neural circuits employ principles like sparse coding, attention mechanisms, and hierarchical processing that parallel deep learning architectures
- Neuroscientific constraints and observations can guide the design of more biologically plausible and efficient learning algorithms
- Understanding neural codes, dynamics, and circuit organization can inspire novel approaches to representation learning and generalization
- Bridging the two fields requires careful translation between neuroscientific measurements and computational abstractions
