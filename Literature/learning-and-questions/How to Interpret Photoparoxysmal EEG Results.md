## Summary
This chapter provides clinical guidance on interpreting photoparoxysmal EEG responses, discussing the characteristics of photosensitivity in epilepsy and how to recognize and classify abnormal EEG patterns induced by photic stimulation.

## Key points
- Photoparoxysmal response (PPR) is an abnormal EEG finding induced by photic stimulation with clinical significance for epilepsy diagnosis
- Proper interpretation requires understanding the morphology, frequency, and distribution of photoparoxysmal discharges
- Photosensitivity is associated with specific epilepsy syndromes and has implications for patient management and counseling
