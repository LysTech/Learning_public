## Summary
This work demonstrates that ImageNet-trained CNNs rely primarily on texture rather than shape for object recognition, contrary to human visual perception. By increasing shape bias through training on stylized images, the authors show that CNNs achieve improved accuracy and robustness to distribution shifts and adversarial perturbations.

## Key points
- ImageNet-trained CNNs are heavily texture-biased, diverging from human shape-centric recognition
- Texture bias can be reduced by training on stylized images that preserve shape while randomizing texture
- Increasing shape bias improves both standard accuracy and robustness to distribution shifts
- Shape-biased models show better transfer learning and adversarial robustness
- Findings suggest a fundamental mismatch between CNN and human visual inductive biases
