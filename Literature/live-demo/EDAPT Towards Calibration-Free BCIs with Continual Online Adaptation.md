## Summary
This paper presents EDAPT, a method for brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) that enables continuous online adaptation without requiring explicit calibration sessions. The approach uses continual learning techniques to allow BCIs to adapt to changing neural signals in real-time, reducing the setup burden for users.

## Key points
- Proposes continual online adaptation framework to eliminate need for explicit BCI calibration
- Addresses non-stationary nature of neural signals through adaptive learning mechanisms
- Reduces user burden by enabling BCIs to work with minimal initial setup
- Demonstrates practical feasibility of calibration-free BCI operation
