## Summary
This paper demonstrates that transformer models can learn to perform Bayesian inference over in-context data, effectively implementing probabilistic reasoning within their forward pass without explicit training on Bayesian algorithms.

## Key points
- Transformers can implicitly learn to execute Bayesian inference on in-context examples
- Models develop emergent probabilistic reasoning capabilities without explicit Bayesian training
- Demonstrates connection between in-context learning and approximate posterior inference
- Provides insights into how transformers process and reason about uncertainty
