## Summary
This paper proposes methods for decoding semantic intent from EEG signals, treating brain activity as a mosaic of distributed neural representations. The work develops techniques to extract high-level semantic information directly from electroencephalography, advancing brain-computer interfaces for language and intention understanding.

## Key points
- Decodes semantic intent from EEG signals using distributed neural representations
- Develops novel approach to extract high-level semantic information from brain activity
- Addresses the challenge of mapping lower-resolution EEG to rich semantic spaces
- Advances brain-computer interface technology for understanding user intentions
- Demonstrates feasibility of semantic decoding from non-invasive brain recordings
