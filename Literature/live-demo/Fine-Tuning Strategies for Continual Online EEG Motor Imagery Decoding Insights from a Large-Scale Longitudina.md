## Summary
This paper investigates fine-tuning strategies for maintaining performance in continual online EEG motor imagery decoding systems through a large-scale longitudinal study. The work provides empirical insights into how models can be adapted to handle non-stationary EEG signals over time while avoiding catastrophic forgetting.

## Key points
- Evaluates multiple fine-tuning strategies for continual EEG motor imagery decoding in realistic online settings
- Provides insights from a large-scale longitudinal study on model adaptation and domain shift
- Addresses the challenge of non-stationary EEG signals and temporal variability in brain-computer interfaces
- Offers practical recommendations for maintaining decoder performance in long-term BCI applications
